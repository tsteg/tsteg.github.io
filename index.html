Biography

In my six years working in baseball operations, I was part analyst, administrator, coach, coordinator, and project manager. With the Tampa Bay Rays, I lived the “Moneyball” experience preparing the organization for the MLB Draft. I worked at the intersection of sports and analytics with goal of making the best decisions possible with all the information and data at hand.

I left the Rays in January 2018 to move to San Francisco and get involved in the midterms. For the last nine months I’ve been managing a State Assembly campaign in the East Bay (nice transition from one low stress job to another). In November, we successfully unseated a two term incumbent despite being outspent nearly 2 to 1.

This blog will primarily be a place for me to research and examine the common questions and talking points I ran into, both in baseball and politics. I’ve had a firsthand look at the inner workings of two interesting industries - and honestly I’m left with way more questions than answers. Hopefully I can dig into many of those questions here, as well as showcase my technical skills and analytical work.

If you have thoughts, questions, ideas, or just general inquiries, please send me an email at tsteggall@gmail.com.
